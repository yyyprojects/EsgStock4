What is an ESG Stock?

Welcome to your interactive guide to ESG investing. We'll break down this important concept into simple, understandable parts. ESG is a framework that looks beyond just profits to evaluate a company's long-term health, considering its impact on the planet, its people, and its own internal practices.

Financial Performance

Many investors believe strong ESG practices are a sign of a well-managed company, leading to better long-term returns and lower risk. By avoiding companies with poor practices, investors can reduce exposure to sudden price drops from scandals or environmental fines.

The Ratings Maze

Beware of "Greenwashing"

Greenwashing is when a company or fund makes misleading claims to seem more environmentally or socially responsible than it truly is. It's a marketing tactic that can deceive well-intentioned investors.

Example:

A fund might be named the "Clean Planet Fund" but still hold stocks in major polluters.

Research the Strategy

Don't just trust a fund's name. Read its prospectus to understand its specific investment strategy and ensure it aligns with your goals.

Look for Transparency
