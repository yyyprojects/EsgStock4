Why Investors Choose ESG

The rise of ESG investing is driven by two powerful motivations that go hand-in-hand: achieving strong financial returns and making a positive impact on the world.

Better Performance & Lower Risk

Companies with strong ESG practices are often better managed and more resilient. This can lead to more stable earnings and a lower risk of scandals or regulatory fines that can cause a stock's price to drop suddenly.

How ESG Funds Build Portfolios

ESG funds don't all work the same way. They use different strategies to select their investments. Understanding these approaches is key to finding a fund that matches your personal goals.

1. Negative Screening

The Challenge: A Ratings Maze

A major hurdle for investors is the lack of a universal standard for ESG ratings. Different agencies use different methods, leading to wildly different scores for the same company. This can make it difficult to know who to trust.

The U.S. Regulatory Response

To combat confusion and misleading claims ("greenwashing"), the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) is stepping in with new rules to create a more transparent market for everyone.

üìù

The Corporate Disclosure Rule

This rule requires public companies to report standardized, climate-related financial risks and greenhouse gas emissions in their annual reports, providing investors with consistent and reliable data.

üè∑Ô∏è

The "Names Rule"

This rule mandates that if a fund's name includes terms like "ESG" or "Green," at least 80% of its investments must actually align with that theme, preventing deceptive marketing.
